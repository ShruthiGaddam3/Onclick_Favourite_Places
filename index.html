Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653, it stands as an epitome of Mughal architecture, blending elements of Islamic, Persian, and Indian styles. Renowned for its symmetrical beauty, intricate carvings, and exquisite inlay work, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, attracting millions of visitors annually. Its iconic dome, reflective pools, and lush gardens create a serene and majestic ambiance.
